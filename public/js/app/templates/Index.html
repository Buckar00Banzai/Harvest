AFRIKA TEMBO

A Visual Journey for the Long Life of African Elephants

AFRIKA TEMBO is a documentary project by visual anthropologist Carolina Caff√© and documentary photographer Fernanda Lenz in collaboration with the African Wildlife Foundation. During one month in Tanzania the team will produce a web series and a visual journal about elephant poaching and its impact on the economy and ecosystem.

AFRIKA TEMBO is an urgent call to share the stories of African people who are witnessing new models of development that can be civic examples to the world. Through the power of filmmaking, we seek to awaken a sense of responsibility for our planet so that we can call ourselves responsible citizens, who participate in solutions to local and global issues that ultimately are connected and affects each one of us.

The documentary production has been made possible by the support of The World Bank but is still in need of funding. The clock is ticking and AFRIKA TEMBO has 30 days left to find the support it needs to complete the web series.
